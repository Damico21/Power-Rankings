Round 7 · The Dust Settles. The Table Shifts.
Round 7 · 2026 Season
Broncic Stay Top.
But Nothing Is Safe.
Round 7 didn't offer clean breaks or dominant performances — it offered contested scorelines, creeping movement, and the slow, grinding realisation that the top of this table is not yet settled. Broncic edged Nethers Men by 31 in a game that looked like a statement until you looked at the numbers. Holmes and Gardens went the other way — beaten at home by FlagDons, dropping points and, perhaps, a little bit of their aura.
The big mover of the night? Shavas. Patrick's mob put up 1,153 to down Phillipou Cumming by 135, and suddenly they're back in the conversation. Nobody saw that coming. The Dark Magicians got the job done against Van Twists, quietly reminding the competition they have not gone away — and Izaks over Cripps Nipps was tight, uncomfortable, and exactly the kind of win that keeps a team alive without giving them confidence.
Bottom line: this competition has no free rides. Every week someone surprises you. This was one of those weeks. Strap in.

2

— 0 10.80 Steady

Rupert's mob continue to be the competition's most reliable side — not flashy, just relentlessly effective. If Holmes slips, DC are ready.

Broncic
— 0 10.75 W 1,136–1,105

📋 Round 7 Takeaways
  • Holmes and Gardens are still #1, but FlagDons just proved they can be beaten — and beaten convincingly. The top spot is no longer untouchable mythology.
  • Dark Magicians are back. Four spots up in one round, a 1,267-point score, and a win against a side that had them for lunch last year. Do not count them out.
  • Shavas are the story of the round. 1,153 and a 135-point win over Phillipou is the kind of result that shifts momentum and scares rivals.
  • Phillipou dropped five spots in a single round. That's the most dramatic single-round fall of the season so far. A response is urgently required.
  • FlagDons beat the #1 side by 122 points. Whatever happens next, Round 7 is the round FlagDons finally arrived.
  • Foopys are in trouble. 8.69 PPR rating, last place, and a 95-point loss to DC. The wooden spoon conversation has officially started.
Seven rounds in. The table has never looked more interesting, more volatile, or more unpredictable. Holmes stay on top. But the cracks are there. In a competition where Shavas can beat Phillipou by 135 and FlagDons can put 122 on the #1 side, nobody is safe.
