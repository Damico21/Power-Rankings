Breaking News · Round 6
Will's Weekly Wagers
Files For Bankruptcy
Well. Here we are. After weeks of telling you the picks were coming, after week after week of "trust the process" and "the numbers don't lie," Will's Weekly Wagers has officially gone under. Not quietly, not with dignity — but in the most spectacular fashion the Ligma League has ever witnessed. The ledger is red. The ego is bruised. The accountant has left the building.
In what can only be described as an act of mercy — or perhaps a business opportunity — Scoonz & Jaco's Snakes & Ladders have once again stepped in as the official sponsor and financial backer of this column. They didn't have to. They really, genuinely, didn't have to. But they did. Because that's the kind of operation Snakes & Ladders is running. Goodwill. Faith. The occasional bad investment.
Round 6 was supposed to be the comeback. The redemption arc. The week where the selections finally landed and the critics went quiet. Instead, it was more of the same — another round of selections that looked great on paper and dissolved on contact with actual football. The confidence remains unshaken, if only because there is genuinely nothing left to shake.
🔒 Lock of the Week — Let's Not Talk About It.
Another week, another lock. And much like the ones before it, this one decided that performing was optional. The Lock of the Week has now strung together a streak that is, statistically speaking, impressive — just not in the way anyone intended. At some point you have to wonder if the lock is actually a very elaborate joke. We're choosing to believe in the system. The system, for its part, has not returned the favour. Not once. But hey — there's always Round 7.

The receivers were at the door. The assets were frozen. Will's Weekly Wagers was one bad result away from being a cautionary tale in a university finance lecture — and then, once again, Scoonz and Jaco's Snakes & Ladders walked in with the chequebook. Bless them. Truly. We do not deserve them.
🐍 Official Lifeline · Presented by Snakes & Ladders
Six rounds in and the Ligma League has moved past polite. It's fully feral now. Broncic are surging, Van Twists are sliding, and the competition's getting tighter in the middle. ANZAC Day looms on the horizon and the Sean Millis Cup is up for grabs. Somewhere in all this, there was a Lock of the Week. We're not going to talk about how it went. Let's just get into the footy.
Match 1
Broncic 1,260 def. DC & Tax Payers 1,236 +24
Broncic announce themselves as genuine top-three material with a statement win. William's mob have been quietly building and this week they made it impossible to ignore — knocking off the second-ranked DC & Tax Payers by 24 points and jumping three spots in the process. Rupe's side won't enjoy looking at those numbers. They were competitive right until they weren't, and that 24-point gap will sting more than a thrashing would.
🔒 Lock of the Week — Let's Move On
Phillipou 1,105 def. Flagdons 1,100 +5
Right. So. Moving on. Phillipou wins — great. But let's not get too excited when the margin is five points. This was one stiff breeze away from going the other way and no one in this league should feel good about that. The Lock — whatever it was, wherever it was pointed — has once again found a way to disappoint. At least it's consistent. That's something. Cam's side scraped through but this kind of form won't cut it forever.
💥 Pummel of the Round
Nethers Men 1,113 def. Shava Girthi 942 +171
Max's Nethers Men are back in business and they did not mess around. A 171-point demolition of Shava Girthi is not a win — it's a message. Nethers Men hold firm at 7th but with this kind of scoring power, they could move in either direction in a hurry. Patrick's Shavas, meanwhile, fall to last place on the PPR table and will need a serious rethink heading into the second half of the season.
Match 4 · #1 vs #4
Holmes and Gardens 1,201 def. Cripps Nipps 1,028 +173
Holmes and Gardens remain unmoved at the summit and they did it with authority. Matthew's outfit put 1,201 on the board against a Cripps Nipps side that had been showing some form, and still won by 173 points. Six rounds. Six consecutive wins. The benchmark of the Ligma League, and it doesn't look like changing anytime soon. The Sean Millis Cup is on the horizon and no one else seems to want to step up and challenge them for it.
Match 5
IZaks 1,112 def. Dark Magicians 1,089 +23
IZaks grind out another win and continue to quietly build a season worth watching. Zak's side are doing it the hard way — 23 points over Dark Magicians isn't a blowout, but it's two points in the ledger and a hold at 4th. The Dark Magicians fall four spots to 9th — a steep drop for a team that's been hovering in the top five all season. Osei's mob need to find something extra before the competition leaves them behind.
⚡ Result of the Round
foopy is lyfe 1,130 def. Van Twists 1,036 +94
Foopys remind the competition they're not here to make up the numbers. A 94-point win over Van Twists — who had been on a serious upward trajectory — is a proper result and Miller's mob climb a spot for it. For Tim's Van Twists, this is a reality check. Three spots down and a loss that raises questions about whether last week's form was real or a blip. At 5th on the PPR table, they're still a top-five side — but this result asks a question they'll need to answer.
💥 Takeaways
  • Holmes and Gardens are untouchable. Six rounds, six wins. The Sean Millis Cup has their name written all over it.
  • Broncic rocket three spots to 3rd — beating DC & Tax Payers is no small thing. Watch this space.
  • DC & Tax Payers stay 2nd but concede their first loss in a while — Rupe's side need a response.
  • IZaks hold firm at 4th. Steady. Reliable. Quietly dangerous.
  • Van Twists drop two — a loss to Foopys raises more questions than it answers.
  • Phillipou are back — jump four spots to 6th. Five-point wins won't win you a flag, but points are points.
  • Dark Magicians fall four to 9th — their biggest single-round drop of the season.
  • Shavas cop the worst loss of the round and tumble to dead last. Something needs to change.
  • The Lock of the Week has been retired for the evening. We'll say no more.
Six rounds done and the Sean Millis Cup is on the horizon. Holmes and Gardens are a freight train. Broncic are coming. And somewhere in the wreckage, Will's Weekly Wagers is being rebuilt — again, on Scoonz and Jaco's coin. Some things never change.
