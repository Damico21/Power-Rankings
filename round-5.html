🐍 Presented by Snakes & Ladders
Five rounds in and the Ligma League is done being polite. The pretenders are being exposed, the contenders are flexing, and Round 5 looks like the week where the standings start telling a story with real teeth. There's a demolition job, an upset of the season, a lock so obvious it almost writes itself, and Holmes and Gardens doing Holmes and Gardens things. Strap in.
Match 1
Van Twists 1,219 def. Broncic 1,201 +18
Van Twists grind out a tight one and silence the doubters — for now. Tim's mob scraped through by just 18 points in a match that kept the scoreboard operators nervous until the final whistle. Broncic will be livid — this was theirs to win and they'll know it. Van Twists bank the two points and move, but they'll need more convincing performances to stay near the top of the pile.
🔒 Lock of the Week — Delivered
Nethers Men 1,077 def. Phillipou 897 +180
Lock of the Week. Delivered. With authority. Max's Nethers Men were hungry, organised, and they proved it with a 180-point dismantling of Phillipou Cumming. We called it. We banked it. We would do it again. Phillipou dropped four spots off the back of this — a rough week that gets worse every time you look at the margin.
Match 3
Cripps Nipps 1,023 def. FlagDons 951 +72
Cripps Nipps finally close one out and it couldn't come at a better time. Hayden's side have been knocking on the door all season — the wrong end of tight results, never quite getting over the line. This week they kicked the door in. A 72-point win over FlagDons is not flukey — it's earned. And for FlagDons, a painful slide down nine spots after weeks of building. This game stings.
💥 Demolition Job
30/09/2023ers 1,162 def. Shava Girthi 1,121 +41
DC & Tax Payers put Shava Girthi to the sword in a clinical performance. Rupe's side are back with a vengeance and this is exactly the kind of statement win that forces the competition to sit up and take notice. Patrick's Shavas came in with momentum but the DC machine had the answers. A rough evening in the office for Shavas — they move up a spot, but this one will linger.
⭐ Pummel of the Round
Holmes & Gardens 1,223 def. Dark Magicians 1,146 +77
Holmes and Gardens do not just win this game — they make an example of the Dark Magicians. Matthew's outfit have been the benchmark of this competition all season and this week they announce themselves in the most emphatic way possible. 1,223 points is a statement. Osei's side will remember this for a long time. When the best team in the league hits top gear, this is what it looks like. Five rounds at the top. No signs of stopping.
⚡ Upset of the Round
IZaks 1,152 def. Foopys 1,022 +130
IZaks bounce back with a thumping win and restore some order. Zak's mob were written off after last week's scare but they answered in the most convincing fashion — a 130-point win over Foopys is exactly the kind of response you want to see from a side with genuine top-four ambitions. Foopys had their moment of glory but this week the competition served up a reminder that it doesn't last long.
💥 Takeaways
  • Holmes and Gardens are on a different planet. Five rounds unbeaten. The benchmark of the Ligma League.
  • DC & Tax Payers hold firm at #2 and win convincingly — the most consistent side outside the top spot.
  • Van Twists rocket from 8th to 3rd — five spots up and a legitimate top-three contender now.
  • Cripps Nipps finally deliver — jump four spots and send FlagDons spiralling nine places down the table.
  • Nethers Men cash in the Lock of the Week and climb four spots. That is the Ligma League's most satisfying result.
  • FlagDons fall off a cliff — from 3rd to 12th in one week. Brutal. They'll be back, but that one hurts.
  • Phillipou absolutely smashed — 180-point loss ends their winning streak and drops them to 10th.
  • IZaks bounce back in style. Foopys reminded that the upset was the exception, not the rule.
Five rounds down and the table is chaos in the best possible way. FlagDons from 3rd to last. Van Twists surging. Holmes watching from the top with quiet menace. This league is not done surprising us yet.
