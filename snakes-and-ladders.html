📊 What is the SALI Index?
The SALI Index Explained

Welcome to Scoons & Jaco's Snakes & Ladders — the SALI Index. Every round, we cut through the noise and identify the two teams that defied expectations the most: one who tumbled dramatically from where they should've been, and one who climbed well above their station. No sentiment, no mercy. Just the numbers, the vibes, and the cold hard truth.

The SALI Index (Scoons & Jaco's Ascendancy & Liability Index) is a performance-based ranking metric that compares each team's actual output against their pre-round expectations. Think of it as the league's official truth-teller — projected scores meet real results, and someone always has explaining to do.

🐍 Snake of the Week
The team that fell furthest below expectations. Could be injuries, bad trades, a horror captaincy — doesn't matter. The slide was real, the SALI index caught it, and now everyone knows about it.
🪜 Ladder of the Week
The team that exceeded expectations by the most. Whether through elite selection, a hot bench, or sheer football chaos — these are the climbers. They're going places, and the SALI index sees it before anyone else does.

Round 1 Edition
🐍 Snake of the Week
Shava Girthis
Came in with all the hype, left with all the excuses. Shava Girthis entered Round 1 as one of the more fancied outfits in the competition — a squad that looked deep on paper and dangerous in the preview reels. What unfolded was a performance that fell well short of the pre-round projection, with key premiums underperforming simultaneously and a captaincy call that aged about as well as a warm pie at the G. The SALI Index doesn't forget and it doesn't forgive — Round 1's Snake award belongs squarely to Girthis.
🪜 Ladder of the Week
DC & the Tax Payers
Nobody put them top of the Round 1 ledger — and that's exactly what makes this so satisfying. DC & the Tax Payers entered the season without the fanfare of some rivals, but quietly went about assembling a score that blew the pre-round projection out of the water. The selections were smart, the structure was clean, and the bench coverage was borderline elite. Round 1's Ladder of the Week goes to the team that proved pre-season noise means absolutely nothing when it actually counts.

🐍 Snake of the Week
Dark Magicians
The spell wore off. Dark Magicians rolled into Round 3 off the back of a promising start to the season — but the wheels came off in spectacular fashion this week. A performance that fell well short of their pre-round projection exposed the cracks beneath the surface, with key premiums going cold at exactly the wrong moment. For a side that had looked capable of mixing it with the top end of the competition, this was a sobering reminder that even the darkest arts can't paper over a bad round. The SALI Index has seen through the smoke and mirrors — Round 3's Snake belongs to Osei's mob.
🪜 Ladder of the Week
Phillipou Cummings
Quiet for a couple of rounds, then absolutely detonated. Phillipou Cummings came into Round 3 flying well under the radar — the sort of team the pre-round projections had filed somewhere in the "should be fine" basket. Instead, they produced a scoreline that rocketed them into the top five, dismantling DC & Tax Payers by 91 points in what the wagers column rightly called the upset of the round. The SALI Index rewards exactly this: when a team ignores the script and writes their own. Phillipou's ladder has never been steeper.
